What is a measurement. Adcock and Collier 2001, three properties.

Property 01

Validity

The number tracks the concept it claims to track. Stance toward climate cooperation, not generic positivity.

Property 02

Reliability

Same input gives the same output. Two coders agree on the same paragraph most of the time.

Property 03

Comparability

Scores compare across countries and years without systematic drift. Watch for subgroup gaps.

Codebook. Three rules that separate codebook from wish.

Rule 01

Operational

Each rule names a feature you can find in the text. "Mentions Paris Agreement" works. "Has a positive tone" does not.

Rule 02

Disjoint

No paragraph fits two rules. Praise plus one concern resolves to exactly one bin.

Rule 03

Testable

Two coders applying the rules to the same paragraph reach the same answer most of the time.

Validation metrics. Cohen's kappa thresholds (Landis and Koch 1977).

Kappa rangeVerdictWhat to do next
> 0.80Almost perfectShip it. Document what made the codebook work.
0.60 to 0.80SubstantialAcceptable for most published work. Check subgroups.
0.40 to 0.60ModerateRe-read 10 disagreements. Refine candidate labels. Re-run.
0.20 to 0.40FairCodebook needs rewriting. Likely a concept-slippage issue.
< 0.20PoorStop. Rebuild the codebook with a co-author from scratch.

Aggregation choices

Mean P(positive)
Average the supportive-class probability. Sensitive to the underlying distribution.
Share supportive
Count argmax labels. Simple. Throws away probability information.
Top-K
Keep the K most extreme paragraphs per unit. Honest when most paragraphs are administrative.

The walk-through. Five things you do every project.

Load the corpus from a stable URL (Dataverse, OSF, your repo). Cache locally. Sample stratified by your time variable so coverage is balanced. Truncate to the model's context window (DeBERTa is 512 tokens). Document the choice. Score in batches on GPU. batch_size=16 is safe on free T4. Save two CSVs. One row-level with the p_positive probability. One unit-level (country-year) with the aggregation. Keep the row count column. Country-years with one paragraph are not the same as country-years with thirty.

Code. Two snippets to keep.

Score, zero-shot in batches
from transformers import pipeline zsc = pipeline("zero-shot-classification", model="MoritzLaurer/DeBERTa-v3-" "base-mnli-fever-anli", device=0) labels = [ "supports climate action", "skeptical of climate action", "does not discuss climate", ] out = zsc(texts, candidate_labels=labels, batch_size=16) p_pos = [dict(zip(r["labels"], r["scores"]))[labels[0]] for r in out]
Validate, kappa and Pearson r
from sklearn.metrics import ( cohen_kappa_score, accuracy_score, confusion_matrix) from scipy.stats import pearsonr, spearmanr kappa = cohen_kappa_score(gold_lbl, pred_lbl) acc = accuracy_score(gold_lbl, pred_lbl) r,_ = pearsonr(p_pos, gold_score) rho,_ = spearmanr(p_pos, gold_score) cm = confusion_matrix(gold_lbl, pred_lbl, labels=["pos","neg","off"]) print(f"kappa {kappa:.2f} r {r:+.2f}")

Validation appendix. What to ship.

Codebook
The full text of every rule. The dated version that produced the gold set.
Gold set
Sample size, sampling method, number of coders, agreement statistic if two coders.
Model and labels
HF model ID with version pin. The full candidate-label list. The aggregation rule.
Validation numbers
Cohen's kappa, accuracy, Pearson r, Spearman rho against gold. Confusion matrix.
Subgroup table
Kappa and accuracy by era and region. Document if any subgroup lags by more than 0.15.
Replication package
Notebook, gold-set CSV, scored-row CSV, aggregated CSV.

Pitfalls

  • Skipping the gold set. No gold means no kappa means no defense. Code 30 paragraphs.
  • Single-label thinking. Always include an off-topic third label. Without it, off-topic text gets forced into one of the substantive bins.
  • Accuracy alone. A class-imbalanced corpus produces 80 percent accuracy from a constant predictor. Always report kappa.
  • Mean aggregation on uneven coverage. A country-year with one paragraph is not the same as one with thirty. Always report n.
  • Forgetting subgroups. Headline kappa hides drift. Stratify by region or era in the appendix.
  • Causal claims from a face-validity plot. A bend at Paris is a check, not an estimate.
